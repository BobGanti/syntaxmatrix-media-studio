We need systems that can be audited, governed, and improved by people outside the walls of the biggest AI labs.

Because without open alternatives, the entire world becomes dependent on a few companies deciding who gets intelligence, when they get it, and under what conditions.

And that should worry every developer.

It should worry every founder.

It should worry every regulator.

It should worry every enterprise architect.

It should worry every government.

It should worry every university.

It should worry every country that wants to remain technologically independent.

The issue is not whether OpenAI or Anthropic are good or bad.

The issue is structural.

No private company should become the sole gatekeeper of intelligence.

No government-approved access list should become the default path to frontier capability.

No developer ecosystem should be forced to build only on systems it cannot inspect.

No enterprise should be told that the safest future is one where all intelligence must be rented from someone else’s server.

That is not empowerment.

That is dependency.

And dependency at this level becomes strategic vulnerability.
